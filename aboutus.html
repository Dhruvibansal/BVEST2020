About BVEST2020

BVEST is the Annual Science and Technology festival hosted by Bharati Vidyapeeth’s College of Engineering, New Delhi. BVEST was conceived seven years back with the aim of providing a platform to students for developing and showcasing their technical ingenuity. BVEST is an extravaganza comprising of a line-up of various Technical and Non-Technical events held over two days, i.e on 5th and 6th November. The types of events organised are varied, ranging from events involving programming, business, debates, case studies, theatre and many more offering something for each and everyone.
BVEST explores the different aspects of Science and Technology year after year and the profound impacts that technology has on our lives. This fest has constantly evolved and pushed its boundaries over the last few years. For technophiles, it is an ideal destination to witness, learn and experience the wonders of Science and Technology.
This year marks the 7th edition of BVEST and is being hosted by the Electrical and Electronics Engineering Department. BVEST 2020 will be held completely online due to the ongoing pandemic. The events that have been designed specially for this grand festival promise to instil and nurture innovative thinking and brilliant scientific solutions in the minds of its participants. This is going to be an incredible virtual experience with a multitude of challenges, all aimed at engaging and educating those who decide to take them on!


Why the SDGs were chosen as the theme for
BVEST 2020

A common roadmap for peace and welfare of the people and the world, now and in the future, is set out in the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Growth, adopted by all members of the United Nations in 2015. It aims to reinforce universal harmony with greater liberty and with a plan of action for people and for the planet. The 17 sustainable development goals along with 169 associated targets are unified and consolidated. While integrating this into BVEST, we are trying to demonstrate that technology can guide sustainability and that we can be associated in achieving these goals by the end of 2030, as stated by the United Nations Development Program. The goal is to be stakeholders in creating a collection of common priorities that address the pressing environmental, political, and economic problems facing our world.

The Millennium Development Goals (MDGs) launched in 2000 were a list of universal targets to be accomplished by the world community by 2015. They set out, among other development priorities, measurable, widely agreed targets for addressing extreme poverty and hunger, preventing fatal diseases, and extending primary education to all children. The shortcomings of the MDGs, however, have been unmet commitments, inadequate funding, a lack of focus and transparency, and a lack of interest in sustainable development. The lack of progress was also because, unlike SDGs, it did not target each and every individual.

But the SDGs vary considerably from the Millennium Development Goals. These were introduced at the end of 2015, which provide everyone with a forum for change. They balance the three dimensions of sustainable development: economic, social, and environmental. The goals target developed, under-developed as well as developing countries. It considers different national realities, capacities, and levels of development. They manifest the moral principle that no one and no country should be left behind, and that every nation should have a shared duty to play a role in achieving a global vision.

This requires all hands-on deck and different societies working together in an integrated manner by pooling knowledge and expertise. Furthermore, Goal 17 aims to facilitate and foster successful public, public-private, and civil society partnerships, drawing on partnership expertise and resource strategies. So, the idea is for us to act as stakeholders to achieve those objectives. The future of mankind lies in our hands. The path to sustainable development has been mapped; it will be for us all to ensure that the journey is successful and that its benefits are permanent.
