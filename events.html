Clash of Code

  • Description

    One problem, two teams! This event provides a platform for your team to participate in a common coding problem and to compete with other teams and fight your way to the top.

  • Rules

    General Rules

    1. In this event, the total number of participants is split into groups of two. Say, Pool A and Pool B. Each team will compete against their assigned pool members in each pool, where they have to find the best solution to a common coding problem. The team offering the better solution will qualify to compete in the same pool for the next round. The final round will see one team of finalists facing each other from each pool to win the event.
    2. The event will take place is in three levels. The level of difficulty and complexity will increase as you begin to move forward.
    3. The first round, which is the easy level, will consist of 5 questions in total and 1 hour will be the total time given to the teams. Teams will be divided into subgroups in a specific pool and each group will get a different set of questions. In this round, selection will be based on the maximum number of questions attempted by the teams out of 5.
    4. The second round is the medium level, in which the teams from the subgroups qualifies. Three questions will be given to teams and the total time for the teams to try out the questions will be 1.5 hours. In this round, the selection will be based on code optimization in terms of time and space complexity, as well as code runtime.
    5. The most difficult round will be the final round and the total time given to the students for the last round will again be 1.5 hours, for 2 questions. In this round, the selection process will again be based on the optimization of code in terms of time and space complexity, as well as code execution time.
    6. A valid team must comprise of 1-4 members.
    7. The issues presented will be related to the 17 SDGs.
    8. Last Date for Registration: 04 Nov'20 11:59 PM IST.

    Judging Criteria

    1. The solution provided by the team should be working and should produce the desired result.
    2. The entire process will continue until only two teams that belong to two different pools are left in the final round.
    3. The team that will provide a better / efficient solution will qualify for the next round and the team that will lose will be eliminated.
